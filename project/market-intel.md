# Tri-Cities Market Pulse (March 2026)

- **Sales momentum remains stable.** The Tri-Cities Association of Realtors reported more than 3,600 closings in 2025—about 3% ahead of 2024—even as supply crept up, keeping buyers and sellers more evenly matched than in the frenetic 2020–24 run.
- **Prices are plateauing but remain near record highs.** Average sold price peaked near $487,300 in February, eased to $477,400 in June, and the median price stayed elevated at about $429,000; the same data shows luxury demand still strong with 85 seven-figure sales last year.
- **Inventory and pace signal a softer market.** Active listings ended December at 938 (still higher than any 2023/2024 month) after topping 1,138 in September, and the average days on market lengthened to 46 days—nearly a week longer than the prior month—providing buyers more leverage going into spring.

**Sources:** Tri-Cities Area Journal of Business, _Buyers, sellers on more equal footing in Tri-City housing market_ (Ty Beaver, Feb. 12, 2026), https://www.tricitiesbusinessnews.com/articles/housing-market-outlook-2026
